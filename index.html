Access to HIV Resources Uneven Across NYC Boroughs, Posing Challenges for Affected Communities

While the Bronx and Brooklyn have higher total HIV and AIDS cases, data suggests that Manhattanites may have easier access to available resources.

By Ray DePaul

December 14, 2023

New York City individuals with HIV and/or AIDS may face challenges in access to City resources, even 40 years passed the epidemic’s beginning.


Inequity in available testing sites varies across boroughs. Manhattan’s ratio of HIV clinics that conduct testing supersedes nearly every other borough, despite only having the third-highest number of cases. Other boroughs, such as Queens, Brooklyn and The Bronx, all have higher case numbers but lower testing sites, with Queens having over 100 fewer than Manhattan.


Even though Mayor Adams acted to raise awareness on World AIDS Day earlier this month through symbolic lighting of famous NYC buildings the color red , the lasting impacts of borough-to-borough resources could prove dangerous for some residents.


“It’s not surprising that Manhattan has the most resources available,” said Meryeme Elalouani, a Pre-Med student at Hunter College who specializes in epidemiology.


“It’s really unfortunate that so many people who are afflicted with [HIV] sometimes have to jump through leaps and bounds to seek testing and treatment,” Elalouani continued.
